My future:

Describe where you're going in life and who you'll become. Tell us about your career goals, education goals, hopes, dreams, etc.

When I first fantasized about becoming a professional opera singer, my role models for the 'perfect singing life' included famous baritone singers such as Bryn Terfel, Gerald Finley, Dietrich Fischer-Dieskau, people who spent their days singing in outstanding theatres, from the Metropolitan Opera to La Scala to the Royal Opera House in Covent Garden, London, and similar venues.

I had no idea how astronomically lucky these guys were (not to mention hard-working!).

Now that I am better aware of the industry, my goals lie in simply performing major roles that I've come to adore, hopefully with reputable companies that can afford some luxuries.

The world has become a drastically different place since I first dreamed of singing for a living. I had no idea that I would one day be living in one of the most expensive cities on the planet. My schools taught me how to sing very well, and I get paid good salaries for the gigs I do, but there simply aren't enough hours out there to make it practical in the long term by itself.

February 2016, enter coding. I realized that there are a couple of perks to getting into this industry now:

  • Web development is an industry reliant on engineers who can quickly achieve fluency in many different languages, a skill I'm already very well acquainted with.
  • Those engineers have bosses who depend on their employees to have creative gifts to solve their daily conundrums, something I am not lacking as a musical artist.
  • This industry is predicted to grow exponentially in the near future with the number of available jobs expanding, not decreasing.
  • The epicenter of this business is in San Francisco, a city I already live near to.
  • The jobs that are out there pay well above the national average, at rates that are several multiples of what I currently make just with singing.
  • Most pivotally of all, it is actually a feasible feat to attain a job within this industry that is truly remote. If I code well enough, I can take my coding career alongside my opera career, from auditions in New York to productions in Minnesota, Los Angeles, Italy, Spain, Japan, and the rest of the globe.

To be able to sustain my opera career with a fulfilling side-career that will allow me to afford all the things in life that everybody wants, like retirement, savings, vacations, etc... that's what I'm really after.

Following Galvanize, I'd like to work for companies who care about my deepest interests, such

  • Language learning (DuoLingo is a favorite of mine),
  • Operatic/Musical industries,
  • Companies which encourage others to become more physically active through their apps,
  • the sky's the limit!

Within the next five years, here are a couple of goals I have vividly in my head:

  • Work with a company that shares my interests
  • Hike the Pacific Crest Trail, ideally around '19
  • Acquire a full rack for alpine climbing, and learn how to do it.

Within my lifetime, I want to perform some of the greatest operatic roles for baritones that exist, while simultaneously earning a good living from coding.

Oh, and I want to summit Everest before I die. No biggie.

HURRAY FOR THE FUTURE!

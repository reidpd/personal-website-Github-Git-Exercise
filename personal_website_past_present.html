Reid Pierre Delahunt

Past & Present

As a child, country music first inspired me to become a musician, but classical music inspired me to become an artist.

I got a lot of help from the faculty of these amazing institutions:

  • Arizona School for the Arts, HS diploma '08
  • Manhattan School of Music, BM '12
  • San Francisco Conservatory of Music, MM '14

As a baritone singing actor, I've morphed into a myriad of different and interesting characters, including

  • John Muir and Theodore Roosevelt in Justin Ralls' Two Yosemites (audio clip!),
  • a clean-cut shoe salesman in Dominick Argento's Postcard from Morocco,
  • a lusty Count Almaviva in W.A. Mozart's The Marriage of Figaro,
  • a swashbuckling pirate (Dancaïro) in Georges Bizet's Carmen,
  • a Greek god in Francesco Cavalli's opera La Calisto,
  • a pair of messengers,
    • Sciarrone, Puccini's Tosca
    • Delmonte, Verdi's Un giorno di regno
  • a slew of drunk people,
    • Bobinet in Jacques Offenbach's La vie Parisienne,
    • Betto di Signa & Pinellino in Puccini's Gianni Schicchi,
    • Happy in Puccini's La fanciulla del West, an operatic western,
    • Frank in Johann Strauss' Die Fledermaus
  • a couple of old dudes,
    • Alcindoro, in Puccini's La boheme,
    • Gideon March in Mark Adamo's Little Women, after the book,
  • and even a few old drunk dudes for good measure,
    • Cinderella's dad in Stephen Sondheim's Into the Woods,
    • Dr. Falke in Johann Strauss' Die Fledermaus
  • along with a plethora of ensemble credits, with characters ranging from peasants to lords, knights to slaves, soldiers to civilians, and everything in-between.

Since graduating from school, I've gotten to work closely with Pocket Opera and Opera San Jose, two fine companies in the SF bay area, and I hope to work with more in the future.

Outside of opera, I've professionally cantored at a number of distinguished cathedrals and churches, including the Cathedral of Christ The Light in Oakland CA, presently serving as cantor & bass section leader since March 2015.

I also have some outstanding composers in my backpocket as good friends of mine:

  • Justin Ralls
  • Eric Choate
  • James Sheppard
  • and loads more!

Behind the mask....

If I had to choose just one operatic role to personally identify with, it would be Papageno the birdcatcher (der Vogelfänger) from Mozart's The Magic Flute, a guy who just wants food in his belly, wine in his goblet, birds to catch, and a pretty Mädchen to have and hold.

I'm a keen outdoorsman who avidly enjoys backpacking, biking, casual hiking, and climbing (my gym club of choice is Touchstone).

I hiked the John Muir Trail after finishing grad school, and now I yearn to hike the Pacific Crest Trail, Gates of the Arctic NP, and one day, Everest (This film convinced me to do it, haha!).

In my downtime I love to cook gourmet macaroni and cheese(this book will change your life), learn new languages (DuoLingo is the best!), and watch movies as a guilty pleasure.
